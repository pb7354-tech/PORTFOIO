Welcome to my academic portfolio on Helical Computed Tomography (CT). This webpage is created as part of the Advanced Medical Imaging Systems (AMIS) coursework. It provides a structured overview of the principles, background, instrumentation, and applications of Helical CT. Helical CT is one of the most advanced imaging technologies used in clinical diagnostics for its ability to perform fast, high-resolution, and three-dimensional imaging. This portfolio highlights the evolution of CT scanning, its technical advancements, and its vital role in modern medical imaging.

Introduction

Helical Computed Tomography (CT) is a medical imaging technique that acquires continuous volumetric data as the X-ray tube rotates around the patient while the table moves through the gantry. This helical movement enables high-speed scanning and accurate 3D image reconstruction. It is used extensively for diagnosing complex medical conditions such as tumors, vascular diseases, and internal injuries.

Background

Helical CT was introduced in the late 1980s after the development of slip-ring technology, which allowed the CT gantry to rotate continuously. Traditional CT scanners used a “stop-and-shoot” approach, capturing one slice at a time. Helical CT revolutionized this by enabling continuous data acquisition, reducing scan time, and improving resolution. The introduction of multi-detector CT (MDCT) further advanced the field by capturing multiple slices per rotation.

Working Mechanism

The X-ray tube and detector rotate simultaneously as the patient table moves smoothly through the scanner. This motion generates helical (spiral) data, reconstructed into detailed cross-sectional or 3D images using algorithms such as filtered back projection and iterative reconstruction. Continuous scanning minimizes motion artifacts and provides isotropic resolution, enabling accurate anatomical visualization in all planes.

Instrumentation

A Helical CT scanner comprises key components such as an X-ray tube, detector array, slip rings for continuous power transmission, a rotating gantry, and a motorized patient table. Modern scanners incorporate solid-state detectors, automatic exposure control, and high-speed data processors to ensure high-quality imaging with reduced radiation dose.

Advantages

  • Enables fast, continuous scanning and 3D imaging.
  • Minimizes patient movement and motion artifacts.
  • Improves diagnostic precision and comfort.
  • Captures large anatomical regions in seconds.

Applications

  • Brain, chest, and abdominal imaging.
  • Detection of tumors, fractures, and vascular diseases.
  • CT angiography and cardiac evaluation.
  • Preoperative planning and radiotherapy guidance.

Future Scope

The future of Helical CT lies in low-dose imaging, photon-counting detectors, and artificial intelligence-based image reconstruction. AI-assisted Helical CT will improve image clarity, reduce artifacts, and enhance diagnostic speed, paving the way for personalized medical imaging.
